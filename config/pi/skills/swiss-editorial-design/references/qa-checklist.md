# QA Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing design direction, layouts, or code.

## Immediate pass or fail
- Is the dominant move obvious within one second?
- Does the design still feel strong in grayscale?
- Does the grid feel real rather than implied by coincidence?
- Is the typographic hierarchy unambiguous?
- Does the result avoid generic SaaS marketing patterns?

If any answer is no, revise before finalizing.

## Structural checks
- Are major edges aligned to columns or rows?
- Are margins consistent?
- Do repeated elements repeat with intention?
- Is there at least one meaningful area of negative space?
- Are section transitions still part of the same system?

## Typographic checks
- Is display type large enough to feel assertive?
- Is body copy concise enough to keep the page editorial?
- Are labels and metadata precise and small?
- Are line breaks intentional?
- Is any decorative font choice weakening the system?

## Color checks
- Is the palette restrained?
- Is the accent concentrated rather than scattered?
- Would the page still work if the accent disappeared?

## Shape and image checks
- Do shapes feel modular and aligned?
- Are images cropped with intent?
- Is there only one dominant visual gesture per section?

## Texture checks
- Is texture clearly optional and controlled?
- Does texture preserve readability?
- Would the design still succeed without the texture layer?

## Motion checks
- Does motion reinforce the layout rather than distract from it?
- Are transitions calm and precise?
- Do elements land on the same structural anchors after movement?

## Final smell test
Ask:
- Would this still look convincing as a printed poster?
- Does this feel more like editorial design than product marketing?
- Is there anything soft, cute, glossy, or overly friendly that should be removed?
- Can one element be removed to make the composition stronger?
