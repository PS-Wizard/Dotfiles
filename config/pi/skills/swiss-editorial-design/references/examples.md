# Examples

Use these examples to calibrate outputs.

## Example 1: AI studio landing page

Input:
Build a desktop-first landing page for an AI studio that feels bold, editorial, and minimal.

Good approach:
- Choose `contemporary editorial`.
- Use a 12-column grid on an off-white background.
- Create a hero with one giant line-broken statement across 8 to 10 columns.
- Place a small metadata block with services, location, and year in the upper right or lower left.
- Use a single vermilion accent on one rule, one word, or one geometric block.
- Structure the next sections as manifesto, capabilities matrix, selected work, and closing CTA.
- Keep buttons rectangular and quiet.
- Keep motion to match-and-move transitions between section states or page changes.

Bad approach:
- centered logo, paragraph, and two pill buttons
- pastel gradients
- three identical feature cards with icons
- soft drop shadows

## Example 2: Presentation title slide

Input:
Create a title slide for a strategy deck about future retail systems.

Good approach:
- Use `classic swiss` or `contemporary editorial` depending on tone.
- Let the title occupy most of the slide width.
- Add a small issue number, presenter name, and date in a stable corner.
- Use one accent line or block only.
- Leave a large empty area.

Bad approach:
- centered title with subtitle and stock icon
- colorful geometric wallpaper
- multiple small text blocks fighting for attention

## Example 3: Experimental culture page

Input:
Design a landing page for a cultural event that should feel more poster-like and slightly raw.

Good approach:
- Choose `experimental poster`.
- Keep the same grid discipline underneath.
- Use a giant numeral or warped line field as the dominant move.
- Allow monochrome imagery or abstract geometry with optional grain.
- Keep metadata sharp and aligned.
- Limit the palette to black, off-white, and one red or orange.

Bad approach:
- glitch everything
- use several effects at once
- sacrifice readability for atmosphere
